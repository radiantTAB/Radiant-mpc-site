01Overview

QuikFlow is a complete business income and expense tracker. It runs entirely on your device — in a web browser or installed as an app — with no accounts, no cloud, and no subscription. Every record stays on the device you entered it on.

With QuikFlow you can:

  • Log income — consulting fees, contract payments, retainers
  • Log expenses with categories and receipt photos
  • Group expenses into client trips and track business mileage
  • Watch a live dashboard of income, expenses, and net profit
  • Generate Profit & Loss and detailed expense reports for tax preparation
Good to know

QuikFlow is the successor to QuikXpense. Every expense, trip, mileage, and receipt feature carries over unchanged — income tracking, the dashboard, and the P&L report are the new additions.

02Getting Started

Opening QuikFlow

Open index.html in any modern browser. QuikFlow loads instantly and works completely offline after the first open.

Installing it as an app

  • iPhone / iPad (Safari): tap the Share button, then Add to Home Screen. QuikFlow then launches full-screen, like a native app.
  • Desktop (Chrome / Edge): use the Install icon in the address bar to add it as a standalone window.

Finding your way around

Seven tabs run along the bottom of the screen. The Radiant logo and company name sit at the top-left of every page so you always know where you are.

03Dashboard

The Dashboard is the default home screen. It summarizes your business finances with no input required.

  • Total Income — received this month, with the year-to-date total beneath it
  • Total Expenses — spent this month, plus year-to-date
  • Net Profit / Loss — income minus expenses, for the month and year-to-date
  • Income vs. Expenses — a month-by-month bar chart for the current year
  • Expense Breakdown and Income Breakdown — donut charts splitting each by category (year-to-date)
Note

Expense figures throughout QuikFlow include mileage, valued at the IRS standard rate. A brand-new QuikFlow shows all zeros until you add your first entries.

04Income

The Income tab is where you record every payment your business receives.

Adding income

  1. Open the Income tab and tap the + button.
  2. Fill in the form (fields below).
  3. Tap Save income.
FieldNotes
AmountRequired. The payment amount in dollars.
Source / PayerRequired. Who paid you — e.g. One Physics.
DateRequired. Defaults to today.
CategoryConsulting Fee, Contract Payment, Retainer, and more.
Payment MethodACH, Check, Wire, Credit Card, Cash, or Other.
Invoice #Optional reference number.
NotesOptional free text.

Editing or deleting an entry

Tap any row in the income list to reopen it. Change any field and tap Save changes, or tap Delete income to remove it.

Finding entries

Use the search box to match a source, invoice number, or note; the category filter to narrow the list; and the sort menu to order by date, amount, or source.

Importing income from a spreadsheet

  1. On the Income tab, tap ⬆ Import CSV/Excel.
  2. Choose a .csv or .xlsx file.
  3. Match your spreadsheet's columns to QuikFlow's fields — it auto-matches common names.
  4. Review the preview, then confirm the import.
Tip

Required columns are Date, Amount, and Source. Rows missing a required value are skipped and listed for you, so a messy file never imports bad data.

05Expenses

The Expenses tab records every business cost, with optional receipt photos.

Adding an expense

  1. Open the Expenses tab and tap the + button.
  2. Enter the amount, merchant, date, category, payment method, and (optionally) a trip and notes.
  3. Attach a receipt with Take Photo or Choose from Library.
  4. Tap Save expense.

Categories are aligned to IRS Schedule C lines, so your reports group correctly for tax preparation. Type a merchant name and QuikFlow suggests a likely category.

Receipt scanning (optional)

If you turn on Receipt Scanning in Settings and add an Anthropic API key, a new receipt photo is read automatically to fill in the amount, merchant, and date. This is the only feature that sends data off the device, and it is off until you enable it. See Data & Privacy.

Viewing, changing, and bulk-deleting

  • Tap an expense to view its full details and receipt.
  • Expenses are view-only once saved — to correct one, delete it and add a new one. This deliberately prevents duplicate-entry mistakes.
  • Press and hold a row to enter selection mode, then delete several expenses at once.

06Trips & Mileage

Trips

A trip groups related expenses for one client engagement — useful for reimbursable work. Create a trip from the Trips tab with the + button (name, client, dates, location, purpose), then assign expenses to it using the Trip field on the expense form.

Mileage

  1. Open the Mileage tab and tap +.
  2. Enter the date, start and end locations, miles, and purpose.
  3. Turn on Round trip to double the distance automatically.
  4. Tap Save.

Mileage is valued at the IRS standard rate (70¢ per mile for 2026). QuikFlow calculates the reimbursement amount for you and includes mileage as an expense category in the dashboard and P&L.

07P&L Report

The P&L tab produces a profit-and-loss statement for any period.

  1. Open the P&L tab.
  2. Choose a period: Month, Quarter, YTD, or Custom (pick your own dates).
  3. Review income by category, expenses by category, and the net profit or loss.
  4. Tap Export PDF or Export CSV to save or share it.

The report lists an income subtotal by category, an expense subtotal by category (mileage included), and the Net Profit / Loss at the bottom.

Important

P&L figures are prepared for management review. Always confirm them with your CPA before filing taxes.

08Expense Report (PDF)

For a detailed, receipt-by-receipt expense report, open the Expenses tab and tap Reports › in the top-right corner.

  • Choose Current Month, Year-to-Date, a Custom Range, or any past month.
  • QuikFlow builds a multi-page PDF: a cover summary, a category summary mapped to Schedule C, a per-trip breakdown, a mileage log, and a receipt-photo appendix.
  • The finished PDF opens in your device's share sheet so you can email it — for example, straight to your CPA.

This detailed report covers expenses only. For income-and-expense totals, use the P&L Report.

10Data, Privacy & Backup

QuikFlow stores all of your records locally on the device, in the browser's own storage. There is no cloud account and nothing is uploaded.

  • Offline-first: once loaded, QuikFlow needs no internet connection.
  • Per device: data does not sync between devices or browsers — each copy is independent.
  • The one exception is Receipt Scanning: when you enable it, a receipt photo is sent to the Claude API to read it, then discarded. With scanning off, nothing leaves the device.
Back up your data

Because data lives only in the browser, clearing your browser's site data — or using Reset All Data — erases QuikFlow's records with no undo. Export a P&L or expense report regularly so you always have a copy.

11Tips & FAQ

How do I correct a saved expense?

Open it, tap Delete expense, and add a fresh one. Income entries, by contrast, can be edited directly.

Why does the Dashboard show $0.00?

QuikFlow ships blank — there is no demo data. The dashboard and charts fill in as soon as you add income and expenses.

Can I use QuikFlow on my phone and my computer?

Yes, but each device keeps its own separate data — there is no automatic sync. Pick one device as your system of record, or export reports to move figures across.

What happens to an imported row with no date?

It uses today's date. Rows missing a required amount or source / merchant are skipped and shown to you before anything is saved.

Does mileage count as an expense?

Yes. Mileage is valued at the IRS standard rate and appears as its own expense category in both the Dashboard and the P&L Report.

Is my data safe if I close the browser?

Yes — records persist between sessions. Just avoid clearing the browser's site data, which would remove them.
