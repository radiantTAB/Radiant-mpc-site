Draft / evaluation tool — not validated for clinical use. QuikDose computes EQD2/BED as decision support, not a finished clinical determination. Features

What QuikDose does.

Two focused ESAPI binary plugins — one for HDR brachytherapy, one for external-beam re-irradiation — sharing the same linear-quadratic radiobiology and the same Radiant clinical-safety discipline.

HDR Brachytherapy Tracking

Per-fraction EQD2 and BED accumulation across an HDR course in a spreadsheet-style tracking layout, with the remaining EQD2 budget back-converted to a physical dose per fraction.

External-Beam Cumulative EQD2

Voxel-level cumulative EQD2 for re-irradiation planning — prior in-Eclipse and Velocity-imported courses summed onto the current planning CT with per-OAR α/β assignment.

Retreatment Constraint Checks

Traffic-light compliance against published retreatment limits — Timmerman SBRT, Jereczek-Fossa pelvic, and QUANTEC cord/brainstem retreatment supplements — that your own policy can override.

Radiobiology, Shown Plainly

Standard linear-quadratic EQD2 and BED with explicit per-fraction dose and time-dependent recovery factors — the same equations a physicist would check by hand.

Runs Inside Eclipse

Read-only ESAPI binary plugins for Eclipse v15.6. They run on the ARIA-authenticated workstation against the open patient — no database wiring, and no PHI leaves the clinic.

DVH Overlays & DICOM Export

Prior, current, and cumulative EQD2 on shared DVH axes, plus DICOM RTDOSE export of the cumulative distribution for re-import and visualization in Eclipse.

Who It's For

Made for the re-treatment decision.

QuikDose is built for the medical physicist supporting re-irradiation and brachytherapy programs — where the question is rarely "what is the dose" but "what is the cumulative dose, and where does it land relative to a constraint that has already been spent once." It puts that math next to the plan, in Eclipse, instead of in a spreadsheet alongside it.

Both plugins are converted from single-file scripts already in clinical-implementation use, repackaged as compiled binary plugins so they install, version, and approve cleanly. The radiobiology is unchanged in conversion — and, like every Radiant tool, QuikDose is distributed as an evaluation build: its output is decision support for physicist review and formal approval, never an automatic clinical determination.
